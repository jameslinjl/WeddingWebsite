J & T

10.17.15

OUR STORY

Shortly after starting their freshman year at Columbia University, Tanni and James met for the first time. Both had gotten involved with Christian Union at Columbia, a ministry and family that would later evolve into Columbia Faith and Action, earlier in the semester, but at that point the men and the women in the group had not crossed the gender barrier and met each other yet. And so at a Christian Union social event in JJ's Place, the two were fatefully introduced... and did not speak a word to each other that night.

Their first conversation was prompted by Tanni (at the time called "Tatianna" by James) being the sole attendee of a praise and worship night hosted by James. After struggling through finding songs they both knew, they settled on DCB's "How He Loves" (it turned out that Tanni actually quite disliked the song, while James was long under the impression that she loved it). Over the course of the next year, both Tanni and James served in leadership for CFA and grew in mutual admiration, seeing the other's love for Jesus and care for others.

This admiration gradually grew into attraction which became solidified in commitment on March 31, 2013. James and Tanni greatly enjoyed their time dating, experiencing the ups and downs and ups of learning one another, meeting the other's family, and having adventures both together and at a distance from one another. However, this finally changed on a fateful Sunday in October 2014...

James Xue, Best Man

He is Chinese-American, studies Computer Science, hails from a suburban town about 2 hours from New York City, has a sister who graduated from Johns Hopkins, and loves Jesus; he is James... Xue. After finding out that he shared a rather unusual number of traits with the groom, James subsequently agreed to be the groom's roommate and best friend for every one of their four years at Columbia. In their prime, you could commonly find these two coding, taking late night strolls around campus, and balling at Dodge. Through all of these experiences and more, James has forever shaped the trajectory of the groom's life into one of more love, more serious thought and reflection, and more of God's adventure. James holds bioinformatics and Columbia's Christian A Capella group Jubilation! dear to his heart, and he will be pursuing a PhD and research career in one of the two.

Karl Li, Best Man

Sometime around the 4th grade, when the groom discovered that he possessed a Gameboy Link Cable, Karl became a much cherished friend in the groom's eyes. Little did either of them know that this was the beginning of a special friendship, rivaling the brotherhood of Mario and Luigi, which would mold and shape them both in those formative years. Karl taught the groom how to be transparent before his peers, how NOT to interact with girls back in middle school and high school, and how to love Jesus with fervor and intensity. With Relient K as their muse, Karl and the groom could often be found in the church sanctuary jamming on guitar and bass as well as loving on their Brothers-in-Christ in various ways. Karl is passionate about business, technology, and the Gospel, and he will be in Philadelphia start his labor in tech consulting in the coming months.

Wilson Hsu, Groomsman

Wilson and the groom met a long, long time ago, back in the days of full day Vacation Bible School and Chinese Culture Camp, where slip 'n slides and grappling tournaments were highlights of the summer months. Over the years, Wilson could often be found teaching the groom to breakdance among other athletic and artistic endeavors, getting him to loosen and lighten up, and filling out the rhythm section of Jamwilkarsones with the groom. Their relationship was not always smooth; it had to endure times of intense drama, anger, passion, and compassion. But praise God that He used these moments in His infinite wisdom. Wilson taught the groom about loyalty and resilience, both of which are challenging lessons. When he isn't serving and loving his ROX and Pi Alpha Phi communities, he can be found playing a variety sports or video games; Wilson will be pursuing a career in tech after graduation.

Ryan Bae, Groomsman

On the first day of college, Ryan was the first member of Carman 204 that the groom encountered. Through his amazing gift of finding free events and discounted excursions in the New York, Ryan brought many adventures to the four members of 204 and beyond. Over their time at Columbia, Ryan and the groom shared countless highs and lows, serious conversations and light-hearted Starcraft matches, tears and laughter. He showed the groom what it looks like to bounce back from adversity; in their time together, he displayed and inspired bravery in the face of his personal fears and obstacles. Ryan can often be found tinkering with and dreaming of the next big start up idea as well as enjoying food from around the neighborhood with the groom and others. He loves solving challenging business problems and looks forward to consulting and entrepreneurship in his future.
